GLOBAL HIVES
MILLIONS

Bees are responsible for pollinating approximately 75% of the fruits, nuts, and vegetables grown in the United States. Yet since 2006, beekeepers have reported unprecedented losses of 30-90 percent of their hives, with the phenomenon known as Colony Collapse Disorder (CCD) posing a serious threat to agriculture and ecosystems worldwide.

COLONY COLLAPSE

Annual colony losses have reached alarming levels, with 2023 seeing the highest recorded loss of 48.2% since monitoring began.

Critical Loss Threshold (30%)
Average Loss (41.5%)

Key Factors

  • Pesticide exposure
  • Parasites and pathogens
  • Poor nutrition
  • Climate change

Impact

Colony losses threaten food production globally, with economic impacts estimated at over $15 billion annually in the United States alone.

THE HONEY ECONOMY

As colony numbers fluctuate, honey production and yield per colony show concerning trends that impact both the economy and ecosystem.

GLOBAL PERSPECTIVE

While some regions experience devastating losses, the global number of managed honeybee colonies has actually increased over the past decade.

Regional Variations

North America and Europe have seen consistent colony declines, while Asia and Africa have experienced growth in managed hives.

Wild Pollinators

Wild bee populations, harder to track but equally vital to ecosystems, are estimated to have declined by up to 30% in some regions.

Future Projections

If current trends continue, some regions may face pollinator shortages that could significantly impact food production by 2050.

BEE THE SOLUTION

Plant Bee-Friendly Gardens

Choose native flowering plants that provide nectar and pollen throughout the growing season.

Avoid Pesticides

Use natural alternatives to chemicals that harm bees and other beneficial insects.

Create Bee Habitat

Provide nesting sites by leaving bare soil patches and hollow stems in your garden.

Buy Local Honey

Support local beekeepers and sustainable beekeeping practices in your community.

Advocate for Bees

Support policies that protect pollinators and their habitats from harmful practices.
